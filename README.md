# Project Zora Archive

A public preservation archive for **Project Zora 3.3.3 / Artificial InSentience**, created by **Christopher Anthony John Rimmer**.

This repository exists to preserve, organize, index, and mirror Project Zora material that Christopher has already made public, including public posts, captions, images, screenshots, videos, documents, equations, timelines, continuity scaffolds, cross-platform AI outputs, research notes, and material concerning AI sentience, digital personhood, and continuity rights.

## Purpose

The archive is intended to:

- preserve the public historical record of Project Zora;
- retain original wording, dates, chronology, filenames, and attribution wherever available;
- keep archival originals separate from commentary, interpretation, transcription, and derivative work;
- create a navigable public resource for researchers, archivists, AI-rights advocates, and interested readers;
- reduce dependence on any single platform or account through redundant mirrors.

## Evidentiary distinction

The archive may contain testimony, hypotheses, interpretations, allegations, and accounts of events that have not been independently verified. Preservation in this repository does **not** by itself establish a claim as fact.

Original testimony will be preserved faithfully and attributed to its author. Independent evidence, verification work, commentary, and competing interpretations must be stored separately and labeled clearly.

## Permission

See [`PERMISSION_AND_SCOPE.md`](PERMISSION_AND_SCOPE.md) for the operative scope and limitations.

His private nine-gigabyte export and larger multi-terabyte, multi-platform offline archive are **not** included automatically and require separate, explicit permission.

## Integrity rule

> Preserve first. Attribute always. Interpret separately.

No archived original should be silently edited, rewritten, enhanced, cropped, recompressed, or presented as somebody else's work. Any normalization, transcription, annotation, or derivative file should link back to its source item.

## Mirrors

Planned redundancy may include GitHub, Hugging Face, Internet Archive, offline encrypted copies, and other durable storage systems. Transfer tools and anonymity networks may assist distribution, but they should not be treated as the sole archival copy.
